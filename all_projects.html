Aulani, A Disney® Resort & Spa

Magic—that is the story of Disney's Aulani. A palatial entrance welcomes visitors to this 21 acre beachfront property.

Magic. That is the story of Disney's Aulani. A palatial entrance welcomes visitors to this beachfront property with its hundreds of luxury villas and hotel rooms, conference center, and pool complex with a feature pool, wading pool, tube watercourse, slides, hot tubs, and snorkel lagoon. Additional amenities include a spa, two restaurants, parking structures, wedding lawn, and kids club. Aulani is a showpiece of masterful artwork and engineering, richly decorated in a style celebrating Hawaii's culture and traditions.

This world-renowned resort and spa is the first of its kind for Disney and the largest for the state of Hawaii. About 1,000 craftsmen raised this exquisite attraction with a two-year turnaround. The expertise and materials for this work were imported from the four corners of the world. The Hawaiian Dredging team is especially proud that our contribution included the creation of over 1,000 jobs for the local economy during a period of economic recession.

Joint Traffic Management Center

An award-winning parking facility issued as a design-build competition in 2010.

Map It: Kakaako learn more

The JTMC houses 410 parking stalls and a bus staging transit facility for 16 buses. This landmark structure is located within the Hawaii Capital Special District and was designed to complement and be compatible with the State's aesthetic and architectural heritage. The exterior façade includes perforated horizontal louver blades, architecturally formed patterned Hawaiian concrete walls, a large entry canopy and hanging planters.

801 South Street Condominium

Located only 1.5 miles from Ala Moana Beach Park, this 46 story condominium tower addresses the critical housing shortage within Honolulu’s urban core.

801 South Street strives to fulfill the State’s mission to revitalize Kakaako with “workforce housing”, targeted for Hawaii’s locals not able to purchase homes due to the disparity between wages and the cost of homes. Hawaiian Dredging is pleased to be part of creating an affordable "live, work and play" community for Hawaii’s working families.

Colona at the Peninsula

Honolulu is growing at a rapid pace and accommodating new residents is increasingly a priority for developers and city planners. A source of revenue for the county and state, new residents are often looking for desirable homes close to the beach and city. The Colony provides luxury condominium housing in the densely populated, coveted region of Hawaii Kai. With over 300 air conditioned units plus parking facilities, the development includes a rec center, pool, spa, and open-air pavillion.

Hawaiian Dredging designed and built the post-tension concrete units for which we received an "outstanding" rating by the owner. Each unit features a central AC system and detailed finish work: wood trim, granite countertops, tiled baths, carpeting, and more. Other features include roads, sewage, drainage, water, fire lines, chiller plants, and utility lines to service the residence. At HDCC we know what a home should be, and we strive to add a touch of aloha to each one we build. We're proud to have made another first-class condo that puts a roof over 300+ heads.

Country Club Village 6

Country Club Village 6 is a 17-story condominium with 269 living units and 514 parking stalls. Conveniently located in the Salt Lake district of Honolulu, it provides accessible housing for workers who commute to the city.

Hawaiian Dredging worked with the developer in designing this building to incorporate a cost-effective “tunnel forming” system which allows the concrete walls and suspended decks to be poured simultaneously. Utilizing this unique forming system from France, along with our global sourcing expertise in purchasing interior finish building materials, Hawaiian Dredging was able to offer the developer a quality, cost-effective solution to Hawaii’s housing market.
