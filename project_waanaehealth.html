Waianae Coast Comprehensive Health Center

The primary care facility for the Waianae coast community. A two-story, 22,494 square foot state-of-the-art healthcare facility that integrates a wide array of adult medical services.

The Waianae Coast Comprehensive Health Center (WCCHC) is the primary care facility for the Waianae coast community. Services include primary care for keiki to kupuna, a 24-hour emergency room, laboratory, radiology, pharmacy, community health outreach, case management, traditional Native Hawaiian healing, adult day care, specialty services, behavioral health, health career training, and more.

WCCHC cares for the uninsured and medically underserved patients. More than 31,000 patients visit annually. WCCHC’s board of directors approved a three-phase construction plan to transform its main campus into a state-of-the-art health care facility. Phase II, the Harry and Jeanette Weinberg Adult Medical and Pharmacy Building, began in 2012.

The two-story, 22,494 square foot building integrates adult medical services with medication therapy management, chronic disease management, behavioral health assessment and counseling, and other care enabling services to reflect the team-based, patient-centered health care home model of care. HDCC's work doubled the facility's capacity and added comfortable waiting areas with Hawaiian décor and art.
