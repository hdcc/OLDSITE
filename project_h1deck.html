Highway 1 Contraflow Lane & Full Depth Deck Repair

Honolulu traffic is ranked among the worst in the nation. The state has hired Hawaiian Dredging to install additional lanes for faster travel times and improve road conditions for smoother rides.

One of the bottlenecks that creates this time waster is the H1 Waiawa interchange. At peak hours, drivers spend an average of 45 minutes idling in their cars. Equally aggravating are the rough, bumpy surfaces that make rides jarring. HDCC's work will shorten ride times with the addition of a contraflow lane between Radford Drive and the Waiawa Interchange.

The H1 westbound capacity will increase while the flow lane operates during peak afternoon hours. The innovative design widens the Pearl City viaduct by over 6 feet without major foundation work. A shoulder lane will be installed from the Halawa merge to the Pearl City off-ramp. Another is being added along the H1 from Waiawa IC after the Farrington overpass.

Viaduct improvements near Pearl City will make rides smoother and road lines more visible. The full-depth repair of deteriorated concrete will extend the life of the highway and provide a safer ride.

The repairs are made at night. First high pressure water blasting equipment demolishes old concrete, leaving a hole and exposed rebar. The hole is filled with high performance concrete that is fast-curing. When finished, pavement markings are repainted and ready for morning rush hour traffic.

Hawaiian Dredging is pleased to be making these changes possible and relieving some of the traffic burden for Oahu residents who commute over the H1.
